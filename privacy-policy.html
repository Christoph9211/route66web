Your CCPA/CPRA rights and how to exercise them

Right to know and portability. You may request that we disclose:

  • the categories of personal information we collected about you;
  • the sources of that information;
  • the purposes for which we use it;
  • the categories of third parties to whom we disclose it; and
  • the specific pieces of personal information we collected about you.

You also have the right to know what personal information we sold or shared and to whom, including two separate lists showing: (i) the categories sold or shared and the categories of third parties; and (ii) the categories disclosed for a business purpose and the categories of recipients. Electronic disclosures will be provided in a portable, readily usable format where feasible.

Right to delete. You may request deletion of your personal information, subject to legal exceptions (e.g., security, fraud prevention, debugging, or to fulfill transactions).

Right to correct. You may request correction of inaccurate personal information we maintain about you.

Right to opt out of sale/sharing and to limit use of sensitive personal information. You may opt out at any time. (We do not collect sensitive personal information.)

Non-discrimination. We will not discriminate against you for exercising your rights. If certain information is necessary to provide a service, we may be unable to complete a transaction without it. Where permitted, we may offer promotions or financial incentives reasonably related to the value of your information (e.g., bona fide loyalty or rewards programs).

How to submit a request

  • Contact us using the details at the start of this document.
  • Your request must provide sufficient information to verify your identity and describe the request with enough detail for us to respond.
  • If we cannot verify your identity, we will not respond to the request.
  • You do not need an account to make a request. Any information collected to verify your request will be used only for verification and retained no longer than necessary.
  • Authorized agents registered with the California Secretary of State may act on your behalf. Adults may make requests for their minor children.
  • You may submit up to two requests within a 12-month period.

Timing and our response

  • We will confirm receipt within 10 days and explain our process.
  • We will respond within 45 days. If more time is needed (up to a total of 90 days), we will tell you why and for how long.
  • Our response will cover the preceding 12 months; for data collected on or after January 1, 2022, you may request disclosures beyond 12 months unless impossible or disproportionately burdensome.
  • If we deny your request, we will explain why.
  • We do not charge a fee unless a request is manifestly unfounded or excessive; in such cases we may charge a reasonable fee or refuse to act and will explain our decision.
